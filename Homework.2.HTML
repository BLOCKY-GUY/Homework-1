Biography of Nikola Tesla

The Visionary Engineer of the Electrical Age

Early Life & Achievements

Nikola Tesla was born in 1856 in modern-day Croatia. He was an exceptionally gifted inventor and electrical engineer who famously developed the Alternating Current (AC) electricity supply system, which powers our modern world today.

Tesla held over 300 patents worldwide. Despite his monumental contributions, he often struggled with commercializing his concepts, leading to periods of financial hardship.

Correcting Historical Rumors

Many believe Tesla invented radio entirely alone; however, Guglielmo Marconi was the sole creator of radio communication. The supreme court eventually overturned Marconi's patents in favor of Tesla’s earlier work.

Scientific Notes & Formulas

Tesla heavily studied magnetic flux density. The unit named after him is the Tesla (T). One example metric equation utilizes magnetic force components, where charges carry indices like Q1 and chemical compounds are labeled like simple water elements H2O to clear away contaminants in his laboratory experiments.

In structural energy math, coordinates were marked as areas with values like meters squared (m2).
